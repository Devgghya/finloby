Business Loan 
Business Loan is a corporate credit facility extended to legally registered entities including Sole Proprietorships, Partnerships, LLCs, and Free Zone companies,
to fund working capital, business expansion, or asset acquisition.
Regulated by the Central Bank of the UAE (CBUAE), these loans are strictly segregated from personal retail lending and are governed by stringent corporate
risk, compliance, and anti-money laundering (AML) frameworks.
In UAE banking structure, business loans are broadly split into Retail Business Loans (Micro/Small, high-volume, scorecard-based, often unsecured) and SME
Corporate Loans (Medium/Larger, relationship-driven, heavily financial-backed, often structured or secured).
1. Retail Business Loans (Micro & Small Enterprises)
Target Profile: Companies with an annual turnover between AED 500,000 to AED 10–20 Million. 
Product Types:Unsecured term loans, Merchant Point-of-Sale (POS) financing, Business Credit Cards, and minor working capital lines.
Business Loan Consultancy Process: 
Phase 1: Onboarding & Cash Flow Scrubbing
AECB Alignment (Dual Scrutiny): In Retail Loans, UAE banks rely heavily on "scorecard lending." We coordinate to run a check on both the Company’s
AECB score and the Principal Shareholder's personal AECB score.
The "Bounced Cheque" Audit: We thoroughly review the last 12 months of bank statements. In the UAE, even one or two outward bounced cheques due
to insufficient funds (EFSR) may trigger an automatic system rejection. We guide client to clean these up or wait out the cycle before applying.
Average Quarterly Balance (AQB) Calculation: Calculate the client’s real monthly transactional run rate. Banks generally benchmark the loan amount
against the client's AQB.
Phase 2: Structuring & Solution Selection
Traditional Unsecured vs. POS Financing: If the client is in retail, F&B, or e-commerce, we guide them toward POS/Payment Gateway Loans. 
VAT Return Triangulation: We guide client to ensure that the revenues reflected on the bank statements match the Federal Tax Authority (FTA) VAT
returns perfectly. There are system in place to cross-verify VAT filings against statement inflows. 
Phase 3: Fast-Track Packaging & Submission
Document Standardization: We gather to verify the standard package required by Dubai retail underwriters: Valid Trade License, Ejari (commercial
tenancy contract), Memorandum of Association (MOA), and 6–12 months of bank statements.
Personal Guarantee (PG) Structuring: We prepare the client for the mandatory signing of a Personal Guarantee and a security post-dated cheque (PDC)
for the full loan value, a standard security mechanism in UAE retail banking as per service provider institutions, banking law and practice
Phase 4: Approval & Drawdown
Turnaround Monitoring: Retail business loans are designed to be fast. We monitor automated underwriting gates. Upon approval, we cross check the
setup fees  and insurance fees before signing the offer letter.

2. SME Loans (Medium & Corporate Enterprises)
Target Profile: Companies with an annual turnover between AED 20 Million to AED 250 Million (aligned with the latest CBUAE SME Customer Protection
Regulations). 
Product Types: Structured Trade Finance (Letter of Credit, Trust Receipts), Bank Guarantees, Overdrafts (OD), Asset/Equipment Financing, and Commercial
Mortgages.
Phase 1: Comprehensive Financial Diagnostics
Audited Financials Reconciliation: SME Corporate loans cannot rely on bank statements alone. We need to collect 2 to 3 years of audited financials
stamped by a UAE-registered auditor. Further, verification is required  that key ratios meet UAE commercial banking credit parameters:
Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR): Ideally >1.25
Leverage Ratio (Debt-to-Equity): Ideally <3:1
Anti-Money Laundering (AML) & KYC Sanity Check: We ensure the Ultimate Beneficial Owners (UBOs) are clearly mapped. Verify that no corporate
transactions originate from or link to sanctioned jurisdictions, ensuring complete alignment with CBUAE guideline.
Phase 2: Credit Facility Architecture
Facility Bundling (The "Toolkit" Approach): Rather than asking for a flat cash loan from institutes, we structure a mixed facility. For example, for an
engineering or trading firm, package:
An Overdraft Line for local supplier payroll.
Letters of Credit (LCs) for global sourcing.
Trust Receipts (TR) to bridge the 60-to-90 day clearing gap.
Collateral & Security Asset Mapping: We identify clean corporate assets to lower the borrowing rate. This includes a registered mortgage on industrial
warehouses, factories, office units, or a lien over receivables (Invoice Factoring).
Phase 3: Information Memorandum (IM) & Risk Pitching
The IM Pitch Deck: Unlike retail, corporate SME loans require a formal human underwriting committee review. We guide Firm to write a comprehensive
Information Memorandum detailing:
Industry outlook in the UAE/GCC market.
Working capital cycle (Days Inventory Outstanding + Days Sales Outstanding − Days Payable Outstanding).
Explicit utilization plan for the requested facility.
SME Customer Protection Adherence: We ensure the engagement aligns with the CBUAE SME Customer Protection Regulation (Circular 2/2026).
We guide firm to confirm that the bank provides a clear, bilingual (Arabic/English) Key Facts Statement highlighting all transparent pricing, variable
margin spreads over EIBOR, and covenants, while verifying that "tied-selling/bundling" of unrequested insurance products is avoided.
Phase 4: Credit Committee Management & Facility Letter (FL) Execution
Lender RFP and Negotiation: Present the IM to multiple corporate desks to all major financial institutions to create a competitive bidding environment on
the EIBOR margin spread.
Covenant Management: Review the Final Facility Letter carefully. Ensure that the financial covenants (e.g., maintaining a specific minimum turnover
through the lending bank’s account) are realistic and will not trigger technical defaults for client during seasonal lulls.

Project & Machinery Finance
While Machinery Financing is treated as traditional Asset-Backed Lending (ABL) relying on the equipment itself as collateral, Project Finance is structured as
Limited-Recourse or Non-Recourse Financing, where the bank relies strictly on the future cash flows generated by the project.
1. Project Finance (Special Purpose Vehicles & Infrastructure)
Target Profile: Massive manufacturing setups, infrastructure developments, or large-scale real estate projects. 
Key UAE Concept: The bank lends directly to a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) created solely for the project, isolating the parent company's balance sheet
from risk.
Phase 1: Feasibility Modeling & Bankability Check
The 3-Way Financial Model: We build a meticulous financial model mapping out the Project's Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Free Cash Flow to
Equity (FCFE). UAE underwriting committees require a minimum Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) of 1.30× to 1.50×.
Off-Take Agreement Structuring: Banks in Dubai rarely fund projects based on speculation. We help secure a binding Off-Take Agreement (a guarantee
from an eligible buyer to purchase the project’s future output) to make the deal "bankable."
Equity to Debt Structuring: We help and guide ensure the sponsors have their equity portion ready. Dubai banks typically require a 30:70 or 40:60
Equity-to-Debt ratio. The bank will mandate that the client's equity is injected and utilized before a single bank dirham is disbursed.
Phase 2: Risk Allocation & Legal Architecture
SPV & Escrow Account Setup: We guide the client to open a project-specific Escrow Account with an authorized Dubai bank. All project inflows and
construction outflows must legally clear through this account.
Collateral & Security Package Assembly: Unlike corporate loans, standard project finance utilizes a comprehensive security bundle under UAE law:
Mortgage over the project land/concession rights.
Assignment of Project Contracts: Legal assignment of the EPC (Engineering, Procurement, Construction) contract and insurance policies directly to
the lender.
Share Pledge: A registered pledge over the shares of the SPV with the relevant authority (e.g., DED or DIFC).
Phase 3: Due Diligence & Bank Consortium Syndication
Independent Engineers/Valuers: Dubai banks appoints an external Technical Advisor (such as a certified engineering firm) at the client's expense to vet
the construction timelines and cost estimates.
Information Memorandum (IM) Pitching: For projects exceeding AED 100 Million, we guide firm to pitch the IM to a consortium of local banks (e.g.,
Emirates NBD, FAB, ADCB) to arrange a Syndicated Loan Facility, mitigating single-bank risk exposure.
Phase 4: Tranche Disbursal & Drawdown Management
Progress-Based Milestone Releases: Funds are not released in a lump sum. We coordinate with the bank's project monitors to release funding in tranches
based on audited, certified construction milestones.

2. Machinery & Heavy Equipment Finance
Target Profile: Construction, logistics, healthcare, and industrial manufacturing firms acquiring yellow goods, production lines, or medical tech. 
Key Dubai Concept: This is an Asset-Backed Loan (ABL) or Islamic Lease-to-Own (Ijara)where the asset itself represents the main security.
Phase 1: Procurement & Asset Verification
Approved Dealer Validation: Dubai banks maintain strict "Approved Supplier/Manufacturer Lists." We ensure the machinery vendor is on the selected
bank's pre-approved panel (e.g., RAKBANK or CBI). Buying from unlisted overseas suppliers requires Letter of Credit (LC) structuring.
Down Payment & Loan-to-Value (LTV) Optimization: We guide to budget for the mandatory equity down payment. Dubai asset-backed structures
generally provide an LTV of 75% to 90%, meaning the client must pay 10% to 25% upfront directly to the dealer.
Phase 2: Registry & Legal Compliance
EIRC (Emirates Integrated Registries Company) Check: We conduct an asset title search. The bank will register a possessory pledge/hypothecation
over the movable machinery on the EIRC registry to prevent the client from selling or re-pledging the machine during the loan tenure.
Tenure Matching: We help to structure the amortization schedule relative to the useful life of the asset. Dubai banks cap machinery financing at 4 to 7
years, ensuring the loan does not outlast the physical depreciation of the asset.
Phase 3: Credit Underwriting & Insurance Packaging
Cash Flow Triangulation: Even though the loan is secured by the asset, the bank requires proof that the machinery will generate immediate revenue.
Present contracts or pipeline projects that require this specific machinery.
Mandatory Comprehensive Asset Insurance: We help to package a specialized asset insurance policy with the bank listed as the Sole Loss Payee. The
policy must cover operational damage, theft, and third-party liabilities common to Dubai industrial zones.
Phase 4: Disbursement & Delivery Order
Direct-to-Dealer Payout: Upon approval of the Facility Letter, the bank cuts a manager’s cheque or processes a direct swift transfer directly to the
machinery dealer, never to the client’s operational account.
Delivery & Asset Inspection: We guide client to Coordinate the delivery of the machine, ensuring the bank’s asset management team physically inspects
and tags the serial number of the asset to finalize the activation of the loan.
