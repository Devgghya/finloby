**Mainland License**
When registering a business on the UAE Mainland (regulated by the Department of Economic Development, or DED, in each respective emirate), you have several legal structures to choose from.
The structure you select determines your liability, the number of shareholders allowed, and whether you need a local service agent.
**1. Limited Liability Company (LLC)**
This is by far the most popular structure for trading, commercial, and industrial operations on the mainland.
Shareholders: Min. 1, Max. 50 (can be individual or corporate).
Liability: Limited. Your personal assets are protected; you are only liable up to the extent of your shares in the company.
Ownership: Following recent updates to the Commercial Companies Law, 100% foreign ownership is permitted for most commercial and industrial activities.
Best for: General trading, e-commerce, logistics, construction, and manufacturing.
**2. Sole Establishment (Sole Proprietorship)**
A company owned and run completely by one individual.
Shareholders: Exactly 1 person.
Liability: Unlimited. There is no legal distinction between your personal assets and the business. If the business defaults, you are personally responsible for the debt.
Ownership & Agent: Expats can own 100% of a sole establishment for professional or service-based activities, a Local Service Agent (LSA) is generally appointed—a UAE National who handles government relations for a fixed fee but holds 0% equity.
Best for: Consultants, freelancers, tech specialists, and small service-based businesses.
**3. Civil Company (Civil Partne****rship)**
A structure specifically designed for professionals who want to pool their skills and operate as partners.
Shareholders: Two or more individuals.
Liability: Unlimited. Partners are personally liable for the debts and obligations of the firm.
Ownership: 100% foreign ownership is allowed for expats. However, like the Sole Establishment, a Local Service Agent (LSA) who is a UAE national is generally appointed.
Best for: Doctors, lawyers, accountants, engineers, and digital marketers.
**4. Branch or Representative Office**
For an existing company (local or foreign) looking to expand its presence onto the UAE mainland.
Branch Office: Can conduct business, execute contracts, and generate revenue, but its activities must match the parent company.
Representative Office: Strictly for marketing and promotional activities. It cannot trade, sign contracts, or turn a profit in the UAE.
Liability: The parent company bears 100% of the liability.
**5. Private Joint Stock Company (****PrJSC****) / Public Joint Sto****ck Company (PJSC)**
These are larger corporate structures intended for massive enterprises, financial institutions, or companies intending to go public.
Private (PrJSC): Formed by a minimum of 3 founders. The shares cannot be offered to the public.
Public (PJSC): Formed by a minimum of 10 founders (except when a government entity is involved). Shares are openly traded on a stock exchange like the DFM or ADX, and a minimum capital threshold applies.


















**Home Loan**
Home Loan (Mortgage Loan) is a secured retail financing facility provided by licensed financial institutions to individuals (UAE Nationals, residents, resident expatriates, or non-residents) specifically for purchasing, constructing, or refinancing residential property.
The property itself serves as the underlying collateral, and the loan is heavily regulated by the Central Bank of the UAE (CBUAE) to ensure market stability and prevent predatory lending.
**The Two Structural Forms**
UAE banks offer two legal and financial pathways to a mortgage:
**Conventional Mortgages:** A standard loan where the bank charges interest
**Islamic Mortgage (Sharia-Compliant):** Structured as a Murabaha (cost-plus profit) or Ijara (lease-to-own). Instead of charging interest, the bank buys the property and sells or leases it back to the customer at a profit margin.
**Home Loan Consultancy Process:**
**Review of Profile & ****Documents **: We thoroughly review client’s personal and employment profile along with all required documents in order to evaluate all liabilities for further procedures.
**DBR & Central Bank Compliance Check:** We calculate your exact Debt Burden Ratio (which cannot exceed 50% of your gross income per UAE Central Bank rules) to ensure your application passes automated bank filtering.
**Review of Critical Banking Requirements: **To qualify for a home loan in the UAE, banks strictly evaluate:
1.  **Minimum Salary:** Usually starts at AED 10,000 to AED 15,000 per month for salaried residents.
2. **AECB Credit Score:** The Al Etihad Credit Bureau score is heavily scrutinise; a history of bounced cheques or missed card payments will stall an application.
3. **Mandatory Life Insurance:** UAE banks legally require you to take out a mortgage protection life insurance policy assigned to the bank to cover the outstanding debt in the event of death.
**Loan Tenure & Age Caps: **The maximum allowable lifespan for a residential mortgage is 25 years. Furthermore, the loan must be fully repaid before the borrower reaches:
1. Age 65 for salaried individuals.
2. Age 70 for self-employed individuals.
**Review of Loan-to-Value (LTV) Caps:** The CBUAE dictates exactly how much a bank can lend relative to the property's independent valuation. You cannot borrow 100% of the property value. The limits scale based on nationality, property price, and whether it’s your first home:

**1. Expatriate Residents (First Property):**
a. Value under AED 5 Million: Maximum 75% LTV (25% down payment required).
b. Value over AED 5 Million: Maximum 65% LTV (35% down payment required).
**2. UAE Nationals (First Property):**
a. Value under AED 5 Million: Maximum 80% LTV (20% down payment required).
b. Value over AED 5 Million: Maximum 70% LTV (30% down payment required).
**3. Second/Subsequent Properties (Investors****): **Capped at 60% LTV for both expats and UAE nationals.
**4. Off-Plan Properties: **Stricter rules apply to under-construction properties, universally capped at 50% LTV.
**Banking Pre-Approval (The "Borrower" Assessment)**
The goal is to secure a formal commitment from the lender regarding the client’s creditworthiness.
1.** KYC & Document Compilation: **We help gather and verify the compliance package based on the profile:
a. Salaried Expat: Salary certificates, 6 months of bank statements, payslips, and continuous employment records.
b. Self-Employed/Business Owners: Audited financial statements, corporate bank statements (12 months), trade licenses, and MOA/Share Certificates.
2. **Application Submission & Liaison: **We help package the underwriting file and submit it to the selected bank(s). We Manage all query-handling and clarify income discrepancies with the bank’s assigned credit team.
3. **Pre-Approval Letter Issuance:** We help clients in obtaining the formal Pre-Approval Letter. Ensuring the client understands its parameters (typically valid for 60 to 90 days), allowing them to confidently place an offer on a property.
**Property Selection & Underwriting (The "Asset" Assessment)**

2. **Bank Valuation ****Coordination:**We coordinate and Initiate and monitor the bank’s independent technical property valuation to ensure the asset's appraised value matches the agreed purchase price (preventing a "valuation shortfall" where the client would have to plug the gap with cash).
3. **Final Offer Letter (FOL)**: We help client in securing the final binding mortgage contract from the bank. We then review the fine print, specifically checking lock-in periods, early settlement fees (capped by the CBUAE at 1% or AED 10,000), and mandatory life/property insurance premiums.

**Business Loan**
Business Loan is a corporate credit facility extended to legally registered entities including Sole Proprietorships, Partnerships, LLCs, and Free Zone companies, to fund working capital, business expansion, or asset acquisition.
Regulated by the Central Bank of the UAE (CBUAE), these loans are strictly segregated from personal retail lending and are governed by stringent corporate risk, compliance, and anti-money laundering (AML) frameworks.
In UAE banking structure, business loans are broadly split into Retail Business Loans (Micro/Small, high-volume, scorecard-based, often unsecured) and SME Corporate Loans (Medium/Larger, relationship-driven, heavily financial-backed, often structured or secured).
**1. Retail Business Loans (Micro & Small Enterprises)**
**Target Profile: **Companies with an annual turnover between AED 500,000 to AED 10–20 Million.
**Product ****Types**:Unsecured term loans, Merchant Point-of-Sale (POS) financing, Business Credit Cards, and minor working capital lines.
**Business Loan Consultancy Proces****s:**
**Phase 1: Onboarding & Cash Flow Scrubbing**
**AECB Alignment (Dual Scrutiny): **In Retail Loans, UAE banks rely heavily on "scorecard lending." We coordinate to run a check on both the Company’s AECB score and the Principal Shareholder's personal AECB score.
**The "Bounced Cheque" Audit:** We thoroughly review the last 12 months of bank statements. In the UAE, even one or two outward bounced cheques due to insufficient funds (EFSR) may trigger an automatic system rejection. We guide client to clean these up or wait out the cycle before applying.
**Average Quarterly Balance (AQB) Calculation:** Calculate the client’s real monthly transactional run rate. Banks generally benchmark the loan amount against the client's AQB.
**Phase 2: Structuring & Solution Selection**
**Traditio****nal Unsecured vs. POS Financing: **If the client is in retail, F&B, or e-commerce, we guide them toward POS/Payment Gateway Loans.
**VAT Return Triangulation: **We** **guide client to** **ensure that the revenues reflected on the bank statements match the Federal Tax Authority (FTA) VAT returns perfectly. There are system in place to cross-verify VAT filings against statement inflows.
**Phase 3: Fast-Track Packaging & Submission**
**Document Standardization:** We gather to verify the standard package required by Dubai retail underwriters: Valid Trade License, Ejari (commercial tenancy contract), Memorandum of Association (MOA), and 6–12 months of bank statements.
**Personal Guarantee (PG) Structuring: **We prepare the client for the mandatory signing of a Personal Guarantee and a security post-dated cheque (PDC) for the full loan value, a standard security mechanism in UAE retail banking as per service provider institutions, banking law and practice
**Phase 4: Approva****l & Drawdown**
**Turnaround Monitoring: **Retail business loans are designed to be fast. We monitor automated underwriting gates. Upon approval, we cross check the setup fees  and insurance fees before signing the offer letter.





















**2. SME Loans (Medium & Corporate Enterprises)**
**Target Profile:** Companies with an annual turnover between AED 20 Million to AED 250 Million (aligned with the latest CBUAE SME Customer Protection Regulations).
**Product Types: **Structured Trade Finance (Letter of Credit, Trust Receipts), Bank Guarantees, Overdrafts (OD), Asset/Equipment Financing, and Commercial Mortgages.
**Phase 1: Comprehensive Financial Diagnostics**
**Audited Financials Reconciliation:** SME Corporate loans cannot rely on bank statements alone. We need to collect 2 to 3 years of audited financials stamped by a UAE-registered auditor. Further, verification is requiredthat key ratios meet UAE commercial banking credit parameters:
Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR): Ideally >1.25
Leverage Ratio (Debt-to-Equity): Ideally <3:1
**Anti-Money Laundering (AML) & KYC Sanity Check:** We ensure the Ultimate Beneficial Owners (UBOs) are clearly mapped. Verify that no corporate transactions originate from or link to sanctioned jurisdictions, ensuring complete alignment with CBUAE guideline.
**Phase 2: Credit Facility Architecture**
**Facility Bundling (The "Toolkit" Approach):** Rather than asking for a flat cash loan from institutes, we structure a mixed facility. For example, for an engineering or trading firm, package:
An Overdraft Line for local supplier payroll.
Letters of Credit (LCs) for global sourcing.
Trust Receipts (TR) to bridge the 60-to-90 day clearing gap.
**Collateral & Security Asset Mapping:** We identify clean corporate assets to lower the borrowing rate. This includes a registered mortgage on industrial warehouses, factories, office units, or a lien over receivables (Invoice Factoring).
**Phase 3: Information Memorandum (IM) & Risk Pitching**
**The IM Pitch Deck**: Unlike retail, corporate SME loans require a formal human underwriting committee review. We guide Firm to write a comprehensive Information Memorandum detailing:
Industry outlook in the UAE/GCC market.
Working capital cycle (Days Inventory Outstanding + Days Sales Outstanding − Days Payable Outstanding).
Explicit utilization plan for the requested facility.
**SME Customer Protection Adherence:** We ensure the engagement aligns with the CBUAE SME Customer Protection Regulation (Circular 2/2026). We guide firm to confirm that the bank provides a clear, bilingual (Arabic/English) Key Facts Statement highlighting all transparent pricing, variable margin spreads over EIBOR, and covenants, while verifying that "tied-selling/bundling" of unrequested insurance products is avoided.
**Phase 4: Credit Committee Management & Facility Letter (FL) Execution**
**Lender RFP and Negotiation:** Present the IM to multiple corporate desks to all major financial institutions to create a competitive bidding environment on the EIBOR margin spread.
**Covenant Management:** Review the Final Facility Letter carefully. Ensure that the financial covenants (e.g., maintaining a specific minimum turnover through the lending bank’s account) are realistic and will not trigger technical defaults for client during seasonal lulls.






















**Project & Machinery Finance**
While Machinery Financing is treated as traditional Asset-Backed Lending (ABL) relying on the equipment itself as collateral, Project Finance is structured as Limited-Recourse or Non-Recourse Financing, where the bank relies strictly on the future cash flows generated by the project.
**1. Project Finance (Special Purpose Vehicles & Infrastructure)**
**Target Profile:** Massive manufacturing setups, infrastructure developments, or large-scale real estate projects.
**Key UAE Concept: **The bank lends directly to a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) created solely for the project, isolating the parent company's balance sheet from risk.
**Phase 1: Feasibility Modeling & Bankability Check**
**The 3-Way Financial Model:** We build a meticulous financial model mapping out the Project's Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE). UAE underwriting committees require a minimum Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) of 1.30× to 1.50×.
**Off-Take Agreement Structuring:** Banks in Dubai rarely fund projects based on speculation. We help secure a binding Off-Take Agreement (a guarantee from an eligible buyer to purchase the project’s future output) to make the deal "bankable."
**Equity to Debt Structuring:** We help and guide ensure the sponsors have their equity portion ready. Dubai banks typically require a 30:70 or 40:60 Equity-to-Debt ratio. The bank will mandate that the client's equity is injected and utilized before a single bank dirham is disbursed.
**Phase 2: Risk Allocation & Legal Architecture**
**SPV & Escrow Account Setup: **We guide the client to open a project-specific Escrow Account with an authorized Dubai bank. All project inflows and construction outflows must legally clear through this account.
**Collateral & Security Package Assembly:** Unlike corporate loans, standard project finance utilizes a comprehensive security bundle under UAE law:
Mortgage over the project land/concession rights.
**Assignment of Project Contracts:** Legal assignment of the EPC (Engineering, Procurement, Construction) contract and insurance policies directly to the lender.
**Share Pledge:** A registered pledge over the shares of the SPV with the relevant authority (e.g., DED or DIFC).
**Phase 3: Due Diligen****ce & Bank Consortium Syndication**
**Independent Engineers/Valuers: **Dubai banks appoints an external Technical Advisor (such as a certified engineering firm) at the client's expense to vet the construction timelines and cost estimates.
**Information Memorandum (****IM) Pitching: **For projects exceeding AED 100 Million, we guide firm to pitch the IM to a consortium of local banks (e.g., Emirates NBD, FAB, ADCB) to arrange a Syndicated Loan Facility, mitigating single-bank risk exposure.
**Phase 4: Tranche Disbursal & Dra****wdown Management**
Progress-Based Milestone Releases: Funds are not released in a lump sum. We coordinate with the bank's project monitors to release funding in tranches based on audited, certified construction milestones.























**2. Machinery & Heavy Equipment Finance**
**Target Profile:** Construction, logistics, healthcare, and industrial manufacturing firms acquiring yellow goods, production lines, or medical tech.
**Key Dubai Concept: **This is an Asset-Backed Loan (ABL) or Islamic Lease-to-Own (Ijara)where the asset itself represents the main security.
**Phase 1: Procurement & Asset Verification**
**Approved Dealer Validation:** Dubai banks maintain strict "Approved Supplier/Manufacturer Lists." We ensure the machinery vendor is on the selected bank's pre-approved panel (e.g., RAKBANK or CBI). Buying from unlisted overseas suppliers requires Letter of Credit (LC) structuring.
**Down Payment & Loan-to-Value (LTV) Optimization: **We guide to budget for the mandatory equity down payment. Dubai asset-backed structures generally provide an LTV of 75% to 90%, meaning the client must pay 10% to 25% upfront directly to the dealer.
**Phase 2: Registry & Legal Compliance**
**EIRC (Emirates Integrated Registries Company) Check: **We conduct an asset title search. The bank will register a possessory pledge/hypothecation over the movable machinery on the EIRC registry to prevent the client from selling or re-pledging the machine during the loan tenure.
**Tenure Matching: **We help to structure the amortization schedule relative to the useful life of the asset. Dubai banks cap machinery financing at 4 to 7 years, ensuring the loan does not outlast the physical depreciation of the asset.
**Phase 3: Credit Underwriting & Insurance Packaging**
**Cash Flow Triangulation:** Even though the loan is secured by the asset, the bank requires proof that the machinery will generate immediate revenue. Present contracts or pipeline projects that require this specific machinery.
**Mandatory Comprehensive Asset Insurance:** We help to package a specialized asset insurance policy with the bank listed as the Sole Loss Payee. The policy must cover operational damage, theft, and third-party liabilities common to Dubai industrial zones.
**Phase 4: Disbursement & Delivery Order**
**Direct-to-Dealer Payout:** Upon approval of the Facility Letter, the bank cuts a manager’s cheque or processes a direct swift transfer directly to the machinery dealer, never to the client’s operational account.
**Delivery & Asset Inspection: **We guide client to Coordinate the delivery of the machine, ensuring the bank’s asset management team physically inspects and tags the serial number of the asset to finalize the activation of the loan.


**Corporate Architecture: Drafting Your ****MoA**** & ****AoA**
The Memorandum of Association (MoA) and Articles of Association (AoA) are not mere incorporation templates; they form the bedrock of your corporate governance and operational scope under the UAE Commercial Companies Law. From a banking policy perspective, these documents are closely scrutinized by compliance and risk management teams during Corporate Bank Account Opening and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) evaluations.
**1. The Strategy: Aligning Corporate Vision with Regulatory Realities**
**Activity Mapping & Dynamic Clustering: **We guide client to explicitly outline all core commercial, service, or industrial operations. Activities must precisely align with the Department of Economic Development (DED) or specific Free Zone classification codes. A mismatch between listed activities and actual transactional footprint can trigger compliance flags, account restrictions, or corporate bank account denials under UAE Central Bank regulations.
**Capitalization & Equity Distribution: **We guide client to define the share capital structure, nominal share value, and exact equity breakdown among partners. This structural clarity is essential to satisfy bank Know-Your-Customer (KYC) requirements, particularly regarding Ultimate Beneficial Ownership (UBO) mandates.
**2. Execution: The Legal & Digital Validatio****n Process**
**Custom Corporate Governance Structuring: **We draft specific clauses managing executive voting weights, profit-sharing distributions (essential for professional civil structures), operational restrictions, board appointment processes, and clear corporate exit mechanisms as per the UAE Government laws and regulations.
**Public Notarization & Digital Authentication: **We help guide the document through formal legal channels using UAE Pass digital notarization or via an official DED Notary Public. This legal execution ensures the corporate framework is fully recognized by UAE judicial, municipal, and financial institutions.

**The Office Setup & Visa Quota Allocation Process**
In the UAE, a company cannot hire staff or issue visas until it creates an infrastructure "envelope."
For Mainland entities, this process involves both the Ministry of Human Resources and Emiratisation (MoHRE) for labor permits and the GDRFA for residency visas.
For Free Zone entities, these workflows are consolidated under the specific Free Zone Authority.
**Step 1: Corporate Legal Foundation & Infrastructure**
**Establishment & Trade License: **The client must possess an active commercial trade license issued by the Department of Economy and Tourism (DET) on the Mainland, or the relevant Free Zone Authority.
**Establishment Card Registration:** Once the license is issued, we help the client to apply for an Establishment Card.** **This officially registers the company with the immigration portal (GDRFA or ICP) and opens the corporate labor portal.
**Step 2: Physical Foot****print Formalization**
**Lease Execution & Registration:**
**Mainland: **We guide client to secure a physical office lease and register it to generate an Ejari Certificate.
**Free Zones: **We guide client to execute a Commercial Lease Agreement for a physical space or dedicated serviced suite.
The Spatial Quota Formula Applied: The absolute ceiling of  quota relies on the net usable square meters on the Ejari:
Default Quota Capacity=  Total Net Useable Area /10 (sq. meters)​
**Step 3: Proactive E-Quota Request & Inspection**
**E-Signature Card:** The client's authorized signatory applies for a MoHRE Electronic Signature Card to link their UAE Pass to the labor system.
**MoHRE**** Quota Request (****Tasheel****): **An initial quota request is filed electronically. The system maps the requested corporate titles (e.g., Financial Analyst, Consultant, Managing Director) against the trade license activities.
**Taq’eem**** (Physical Site Inspection):** A field inspector from MoHRE or the Free Zone will physically visit the office. They inspect the premises to confirm it is a functional business environment equipped with desks, IT infrastructure, utilities, and corporate signage.
**Approval & Allocation: **Upon a successful inspection, the quota is updated on the MoHRE portal, allowing the company to legally begin issuing work permits.


**The Corporate Bank Account Opening Process**
Corporate banking onboarding is highly regulated by CBUAE Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Combating the Financing of Terrorism (CFT) laws. **A business cannot open a traditional corporate bank account using a "Flexi-Desk" if they represent a complex consultancy model; banks require physical substance.**
**Step 1: Building the "Substance" Ca****se File**
Before approaching a bank, we prepare the client for a comprehensive compliance dossier.
**Corporate Documents:** Trade License, Share Certificates, Memorandum & Articles of Association (MoA/AoA), and full Ultimate Beneficial Owner (UBO) declarations (required under Cabinet Decision No. 109).
**Proof of Onshore Physicality:** The bank's risk department will explicitly demand a copy of the Ejari Certificate / Free Zone Lease and recent utility bills (DEWA/Empower).
**Signatory Identification: **Passports, valid UAE Visas, and physical Emirates IDs of all partners, shareholders holding 25% or more, and authorized bank signatories.
**Step 2: Demonstrating Financial & Professional Legitimacy**
**Corporate Business Plan:** A structured deck outlining the client's financial advisory scope, corporate target market, and projected 12-month cash flows.
**Source of Wealth & Financial History: **6 months of corporate bank statements from the shareholders' existing/previous entities or 6 months of personal bank statements demonstrating the origin of the initial corporate capital.
**Proof of Business Activity:** Drafts or signed copies of client agreements, consultancy service contracts, or letters of intent from prospective regional clients.
**Step 3: Banking Selection & Minimum Balance Tiers**
We guide the client to choose a bank that aligns with their initial liquidity profile.
**Step 4: The KYC Interview and O****n-Site Verification**
The Compliance Interview: The bank's Relationship Manager holds a face-to-face Know Your Customer (KYC) interview with the main authorized signatory to verify the transaction details outlined in the application.
Physical Audits: Major national banks routinely send field compliance agents to take geo-tagged photographs of the physical office space to ensure the company matches its declared operational footprint.
Activation: Once compliance clears the file , the IBAN is issued, and the client deposits the required initial minimum balance to activate the account.
